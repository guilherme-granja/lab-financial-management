# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Small household/family group tracking shared finances together. Checked casually, a few times a week — not a daily power-user workflow. Multiple people may view or enter transactions, accounts, categories, and goals, so shared clarity (unambiguous labels, consistent categorization) matters more than personalization for one user.

## Product Purpose

Lab Financial Management tracks accounts, transactions, categories, tags, goals, and duplicates for a family, giving a single shared source of truth on where money is and where it's going. Success looks like: at a glance, anyone in the family can see account balances, recent activity, and progress toward goals, and trust the numbers without cross-checking a spreadsheet.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, trustworthy. Numbers do the talking — no flash, no forced gamification. Voice is plain and direct (Portuguese-language UI, matching existing copy). Confidence comes from consistency and correctness, not decoration.

## Anti-references

- No playful/gamified fintech-app tone (badges, confetti, mascots) — this is a shared ledger, not a consumer growth app.
- No dense trading-terminal aesthetic — this is casual household use, not power-user finance software.
- No cheerful SaaS-cream marketing look — this is a product surface, not a landing page.

## Design Principles

1. **Numbers first, chrome second** — balances, deltas, and totals are the primary content; UI framing stays quiet.
2. **Shared legibility** — every label, category, and status must be unambiguous to any family member, not just whoever entered the data.
3. **Consistency over cleverness** — same interaction and visual pattern for the same kind of action across Accounts, Transactions, Categories, Goals, Tags.
4. **Trustworthy by default** — loading/error/empty states are always explicit (per CLAUDE.md convention); never show stale or ambiguous numbers.
5. **Calm dark theme** — the existing indigo-on-dark palette is the brand; extend it, don't fight it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, large text ≥3:1, visible focus states, keyboard-navigable forms and dialogs. No specific additional accommodations requested.
